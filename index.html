code park

aims to prepare high school students in under-invested communities for careers in web technology.

In our after-school classes, students focus on fundamental coding skills and work on real world projects. They get the hands-on experience they need to hit the ground running in this exciting and rewarding line of work.

The Labor Department slots tech jobs among the fastest-growing this decade, but it's not just hiring demand that makes this the industry to watch. U.S. News' 11 Best Technology Jobs of 2015 also boast an alluring yin-yang of high salaries and low unemployment rates.

US News

our students

will learn and build small projects with JavaScript, HTML, and CSS in Fall 2015.

Armed with the fundamentals, they will tackle Capstone projects in Spring 2016. The projects will be built pro bono for local non-profits and businesses. Particularly motivated and dedicated students will have the opportunity to continue learning in summer internships.
